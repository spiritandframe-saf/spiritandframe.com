The Studio

Memory lives
in layers

Spirit & Frame is a digital memory studio devoted to preserving everyday life with intention.

When I was a child, my favorite thing to do was sit with photo albums — slowly turning pages, studying faces, tracing moments that existed before I could name them. As a teenager, that instinct turned into practice: curating albums for myself, my friends, and my family. That reverence never left.

Today, Spirit & Frame is an extension of that same impulse — to gather moments, honor them, and give them a place to live. I work with the photographs you already have and transform them into curated keepsakes that feel warm, lived-in, and worthy of being passed down.

This work is rooted in community, family, and legacy. It honors the way memory lives in Black households: layered, joyful, imperfect, sacred.

A Few Things to Know

This is not a rush service.

Spirit & Frame work is intentional, reflective, and carefully curated. Turnaround times vary based on scope and season, and all projects are scheduled with care.

Every project begins with a questionnaire.

Once your form is reviewed, you'll receive next steps, availability, and pricing details. Submitting a form does not automatically secure a spot.

What kinds of photos can I submit?

Digital photos from your phone, camera, or scanned prints. Images do not need to be professionally taken or edited. Screenshots and heavily filtered images are discouraged when possible.

This is a collaborative process.

Clients are invited to share stories, context, and preferences, but final creative direction is guided by Spirit & Frame's eye and process.

Privacy matters here.

All images and stories are handled with discretion and respect. Your memories are never shared without explicit permission.

This work may stir emotion.

Memory is powerful. It's normal for projects to bring up nostalgia, tenderness, or reflection. That's part of the process — and it's held with care.
